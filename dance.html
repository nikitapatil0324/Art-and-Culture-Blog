Our Indian dance section celebrates the rich and diverse dance traditions of India. From classical forms like Bharatanatyam and Kathak to folk dances and regional styles, we explore the captivating world of Indian dance.

The Grace of Bharatanatyam

Bharatanatyam is one of the oldest and most popular classical dance forms from South India. In this article, we delve into the grace, technique, and storytelling aspects of Bharatanatyam.

Bharatanatyam is characterized by its intricate footwork, expressive hand gestures (mudras), facial expressions, and elaborate costumes. It is a highly disciplined and expressive dance form that combines rhythmic movements with storytelling, often drawing inspiration from Hindu mythology.

Apart from Bharatanatyam, India is home to a multitude of other classical dance forms such as Kathak, Odissi, Manipuri, Kathakali, and Mohiniyattam, each originating from different regions of the country and with its unique style and repertoire.

In addition to classical dance forms, India also boasts a diverse range of folk dances that reflect the cultural diversity of various states and communities. From Bhangra in Punjab to Garba in Gujarat and Bihu in Assam, these energetic and colorful folk dances showcase the vibrant traditions and celebrations of different regions.
